About Jharkhand

Jharkhand — carved with forests, undulating plateaus, and seasonal rivers — is a land of tribal heritage, spiritual sites and nature reserves. Whether you're seeking adventure, wildlife or cultural immersion, Jharkhand has a calm yet wild charm.

  • • Capital: Ranchi
  • • Famous for: Waterfalls (Hundru, Jonha), Netarhat plateau, Betla National Park, Deoghar temple.
  • • Best time to visit: September to March.

Top Places to Visit

Handpicked sights you shouldn't miss.

Deoghar (Baba Baidyanath Temple)

A major pilgrimage town famed for the ancient Baba Baidyanath temple complex.

Netarhat

Known as the 'Queen of Chotanagpur', Netarhat is famous for sunrise points, forests and serene viewpoints.

Betla National Park

Part of the Palamau Tiger Reserve offering safaris, lush forests and waterfalls.

Hundru Falls

A scenic waterfall on the Subarnarekha River, ideal for day trips and photography.

Parasnath Hill

A sacred Jain pilgrimage site with tranquil trekking routes and forested trails.

Jonha Falls

A dramatic fall near Ranchi offering viewpoints and picnic spots.

Sample Itineraries

Weekend Escape (2 days)

  • Day 1: Ranchi — Hundru Falls, Jonha Falls, evening local market.
  • Day 2: Ranchi to Deoghar (temple visit) or nearby viewpoints.

Nature & Wildlife (3 days)

  • Day 1: Arrive at Ranchi — local sightseeing.
  • Day 2: Betla National Park (safari) + Palamau sightseeing.
  • Day 3: Netarhat sunrise and viewpoints.

Cultural Trail (4 days)

  • Day 1: Deoghar temple & rituals.
  • Day 2: Parasnath trekking & temples.
  • Day 3: Local tribal villages & crafts.
  • Day 4: Leisure day / cultural shows.

Travel Tips

  • • Carry lightweight rain gear during monsoon season (Jun–Sep).
  • • Respect local customs — many sites are religious or tribal.
  • • Book safaris and park permits in advance for Betla National Park.
  • • Use local guides for trekking and village visits.
